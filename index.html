What Is "African" Food In New York?

The annual African Restaurant week festival is taking place October 8th weekend in Flatbush, Brooklyn marking the 8th year of the African fashion, music, food and cultural festival. As a Ugandan born New Yorker I was excited to go on their website to find food from my home being featured, but since I have yet to find any Ugandan restaurants in New York City I shouldnt have been surprised at the lack of options. With Sub Saharan Africans making up only 4.5% of immigrant population and New York and with New York not being a notable diasporic location for Ugandans and East Africans in general, the lack of representation in New York's "African" cuisine is expected.

So which countries are represented?

As of 2019, only 21,718 of New York's 468,820 foriegn born population are African, that is only 4.6% according to social explorer's (2019) estimates . Ethiopians are the most represented East African country in New York and still only make up 0.3% of New York City's foreign born population. But let me stop harping on my little piece of Africa, any African cultural representation in New York is wonderful to see.

Here are the top five home countries of foreign born Africans and how they will be represented at this years festival (country demographic data is from social explorer (2019) estimates):

NIGERIA

2,204 foreign born in New York and 0.5% of New York's total foreign born population. 4 of the 119 restaurants represented are Nigerian this includes Africana Restaurant Root & Bone, Amy Ruth's and Cholate Restaurant & Bar, all in Harlem with Peri Per Grill House and Amandla in Brooklyn.

SOUTH AFRICA

1,985 foreign born in New York and 0.4% of New York's total foreign born population. 5 of the 119 restaurants represented are South African, this includes Africana Restaurants in Jamaica, queens, Divine Flavored catoring in Brooklyn, Brooklyn Suya, and Buka New York, both also in queens

ETHIOPIA

1,518 foreign born in New York and 0.3% of New York's total foreign born population. 5 of the 119 restaurants represented are Ethiopian, this includes Ghenet in Brooklyn, Awash in West Harlem, Queen of Sheba in Manhattan's Hells Kitchen, Meskerem in lower Manhattan, and Bunna Cafe in Brooklyn.

GHANA

1,348 foreign born in New York and 0.3% of New York's total foreign born population. 0 of the 119 restaurants represented are Ghanaian.

EGYPT

1,266 foreign born in New York and || 0.3% of New York's total foreign born population. 0 of the 119 restaurants represented are Egyptian.
